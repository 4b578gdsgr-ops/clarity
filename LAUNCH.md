## 1. MTBR / Pinkbike Forum Post

**Title:** I built a free tool to show where cycling brands spend their political money — here's why

---

A few years ago I had a bike shop. I lost it. That's the short version.

The longer version involves a pandemic, a lease, some bad timing, and a lot of nights wondering if I'd made a mistake dedicating my life to this community. I won't get into all of it. But losing that shop didn't kill my belief that the outdoor community is one of the most values-driven groups of people on earth — people who actually care about where their money goes.

So I kept going. I run a small 501(c)(3) called One Love Outdoors, and over the past several months I've been quietly building something I wish existed when I was selling bikes.

It's called **Love Over Money**.

You type in a company — Specialized, Trek, SRAM, Shimano, Fox, Santa Cruz, Cannondale, whoever — and it shows you:

- Their **Karma Score** (0–100) based on political spending, corporate transparency, foreign ties, and ethics
- How much they spend on lobbying and PAC donations, and which politicians/parties get it
- Whether they're owned by foreign interests or private equity
- What their opacity score is (how hard they make it to find this stuff)
- Alternatives, if you want to spend your money differently

It works for cycling brands, outdoor companies, and publicly traded corporations. Search by name or ticker symbol. It's free. It's nonpartisan — the data comes from FEC filings and public records, not my opinions.

I'm not telling you who to buy from. I just think you deserve to know.

**https://loveovermoney.oneloveoutdoors.org**

Currently covers 65+ companies with 200+ brands and subsidiaries searchable. I'm adding more every week. If a brand you care about isn't there yet, there's a request form at the bottom of any search.

This is a One Love Outdoors 501(c)(3) project. No ads, no affiliate links, no axe to grind — just a tool for people who want to ride with a clear conscience.

Ride on.

## 3. Reddit r/MTB Post

**Title:** I built a free tool to look up the political spending and ethics scores of cycling brands — Specialized, Trek, SRAM, Fox, etc.

---

Background: I had a bike shop. Lost it a few years back — combination of bad timing and worse luck. I've been scraping by since, running a small nonprofit called One Love Outdoors.

During that time I kept thinking about something that bothered me when I was selling bikes: customers would ask me "is this a good company?" and I genuinely didn't know beyond vibes and reputation. I knew Patagonia gave back. I knew some other brands had sketchy ownership. But the actual data — political donations, lobbying spend, foreign ownership, corporate opacity — was buried in FEC filings and 10-Ks most people don't have time to read.

So I built a thing to surface it.

**Love Over Money** — https://loveovermoney.oneloveoutdoors.org

You search any company (by name or ticker) and get:

- A **Karma Score** (0–100) built from political spending, transparency, foreign ties, and ethics
- Actual dollar amounts for lobbying and PAC spending, with party splits
- Who the top political recipients are
- Foreign ownership / private equity flags
- An opacity score (how hard they try to hide this stuff)
- Ethical alternatives if you want options

Right now it covers 65+ companies with 200+ brands searchable — focused on cycling (Specialized, Trek, SRAM, Shimano, Fox, Santa Cruz, Cannondale, Ibis, Pivot, Evil, Transition, Maxxis, and more), outdoor (Patagonia, REI, Black Diamond, Osprey), and major corporations.

It's nonpartisan. The data is from public records. I'm not here to tell you where to spend your money — just to make the information easier to find.

This is a One Love Outdoors 501(c)(3) project. No ads, no affiliate links, free forever.

If a brand isn't there yet, there's a request form. I'm adding companies every week.

Happy to answer questions about the methodology or how scores are calculated.
